The Gay Panic

A brief history of the Gay Panic in the late 1900's, and its relevance to the present day Trans Panic.

(source: Buzzfeed News)

The phrase "Gay Panic" refers to homophobic fearmongering and violence. Gay panic and trans panic have been used as a legal defense for excusing murder and violence against queer people, especially queer people of color.

It wasn't until 1973 that being gay was removed from the DSM as a mental disorder. For trans people to access hormones (and get insurers to pay for it), a mental health diagnosis of gender dysphoria is still required.

Despite this change in the DSM, being gay or queer was still seen by many as a mental disorder. More extreme reactionaries bought into and pushed rhetoric that accused gay men of being pedophiles and sex perverts who would come for your children in the bathrooms.

Recent conversations in the media surrounding trans people have eerily mirrored the same panic towards gay men in the 20th century. Any conversation about gender identity or sexual orientation in schools is made out to be grooming.

Despite narratives pushed by media, most folks don't really care about someone's gender identity or sexual orientation. While I've been asked many interesting questions about being trans, most of these questions simply come from a place of unfamiliarity.

While many people feel completely powerless to create change in the face of such brutal oppression, there are a number of actions we can take in our local communities to encourage social and political change.

  • Get to know your neighbors
    • Getting to know the people in your community can help you form the relationships necessary to accomplish larger goals
  • Help your friends and family understand this history
    • Many people don't understand just how pervasive and systemic anti-LGBT ideologies have been
  • Encourage local businesses and institutions to adopt gender neutral bathrooms as the default
    • Having an extra gender neutral bathroom amongst plentiful gendered bathrooms often makes trans people feel othered when they're trying to go about a normal day
  • Vocally denounce and reject homophobic and transphobic ideas and viewpoints
    • We make our communities safer by standing up for each other
